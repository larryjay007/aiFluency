# Proof Statement-Week 1

I catch machine learning results that look good but are quietly wrong, before they ship. 
I'm proving this to a startup founder who needs to trust a model's output but doesn't have 
the ML background to know if it's actually reliable, so they'll email me to have it checked.

**Why this needs to exist:** A CV lists "machine learning" as a skill; it can't show a 
founder the moment a model's result gets caught and questioned instead of blindly trusted, 
this portfolio can.
